# Pattern & Setting Explanations

## Sparse Infill Pattern: Why Gyroid?

**Available patterns:** Grid, Lines, Triangles, Tri-Hexagon, Cubic, Support Cubic, Octet, Quarter Cubic, Honeycomb, 3D Honeycomb, Gyroid, Hilbert Curve, Archimedean Chords, Octagram Spiral, Adaptive Cubic, Aligned Rectilinear, Lightning, Cross, Cross 3D

**BEST for quality:** `Gyroid`
- **Why:** Best strength-to-weight ratio
- Prints without crossing paths (smoother)
- Excellent for mechanical parts
- Natural curved geometry = no stress concentrators
- Better than Grid/Cubic for quality prints

**Alternative:** `Cubic` (also excellent for strength)
**For PLA decorative:** `Gyroid` or `Grid` both work
**For PA6-CF structural:** `Gyroid` (MANDATORY for your 300g print!)

## Top/Bottom/Internal Surface Patterns

**Available for tops:** Monotonic, Monotonic Lines, Aligned Rectilinear, Concentric, Hilbert Curve, Archimedean Chords, Octagram Spiral

**BEST for quality:** `Monotonic`
- **Why:** Smoothest surface finish
- Eliminates visible line crossings
- Professional appearance
- No seams in surfaces
- Better than Concentric (which can have ridges)

**Top surface pattern:** `Monotonic` âœ“
**Bottom surface pattern:** `Monotonic` âœ“  
**Internal solid infill pattern:** `Monotonic` âœ“

## Brim Type: Which to Choose?

**Options:**
- **Auto brim:** Automatically adds brim where model needs it
- **Outer brim only:** Brim around outside only
- **Inner brim only:** Brim in holes/openings only
- **Outer and inner brim:** Both
- **Painted:** Manual control (advanced)
- **No brim:** No brim

**BEST for your use:** `Auto brim`
- **Why:** Automatic detection of where brim needed
- Adds brim to small contact areas automatically
- Saves material vs "outer and inner" everywhere
- Smarter than manual selection

**For PA6-CF:** Auto brim with 10mm width = maximum anti-warp protection
**For TPU:** Auto brim with 5mm width = adequate adhesion without being too hard to remove

## Infill Combination: Why Disabled?

**What it does:** Combines multiple sparse infill layers into one thicker layer
- **Enabled:** Faster but slightly weaker (combines 2-3 layers)
- **Disabled:** Slower but MAXIMUM quality & strength

**For best quality:** `Disabled` âš ï¸
- **Why:** Every layer printed = strongest bond
- No skipping layers
- Better for critical parts
- You said time doesn't matter!

## Prime Tower Settings (Multi-Material)

**When to enable:** If using AMS or multi-color/multi-material
**Width:** `60mm` is optimal for AMS purging
**Brim:** `3-5mm` helps tower stay stuck
**Internal ribs:** `Enabled` makes tower stronger
**Max speed:** `80mm/s` for PLA, `60mm/s` for PA6-CF
